Galactics

Galaxy (ancient Greek γᾰλαξίας "Milky Way" from other Greek γάλα, γάλακτος "milk") is a gravitationally bound system of stars, star clusters, interstellar gas and dust, dark matter, and planets. All objects in the galaxy participate in motion relative to a common center of mass

Milky Way

The Milky Way is a barred spiral galaxy that contains the Earth and the solar system. The radius of the stellar disk of the Milky Way and the radius of the Galaxy are 16 kiloparsecs. The total mass of the Galaxy, taking into account dark matter, is estimated as 1–2⋅10¹² M

Andromeda Galaxy

The Andromeda Galaxy is a spiral galaxy in the constellation Andromeda. Its diameter is 47 kiloparsecs, which is larger than that of our Galaxy, and it contains several times more stars than the Milky Way.

Galactic Cart Wheel

The Cartwheel, also known as ESO 350-40, is a lenticular and ring-shaped galaxy about 500 million light-years from Earth in the constellation Sculptor. Its estimated diameter is 150,000 light years, and its mass is about 2.9–4.8 × 10⁹ solar masses. It rotates at a speed of 217 km/s

Dwarf galaxy in Canis Major

The Canis Major Dwarf Galaxy is a hypothetical Local Group galaxy located in the constellation Canis Major. The galaxy contains a relatively large percentage of red giant stars, and is estimated to contain one billion stars.
