Stars

A star is a massive self-luminous celestial body, consisting of gas and plasma, in which thermonuclear reactions take place, have taken place or will take place. The closest star to the Earth is the Sun, other stars in the night sky look like points of different brightness, maintaining their relative position.

Sun

The Sun (astro. ☉) is one of the stars in our Galaxy (the Milky Way) and the only star in the solar system. Other objects of this system revolve around the Sun: planets and their satellites, dwarf planets and their satellites, asteroids, meteoroids, comets and cosmic dust.

Canopus

Canopus is a star in the southern hemisphere of the sky, the brightest in the constellation Carina and the second brightest star in the sky with a visible brightness of −0.72ᵐ.

Rigil

Rigel is one of the brightest stars in the sky, a near-equatorial star, β Orion. Blue-white supergiant. The name in Arabic means "foot". It has a visual magnitude of 0.12ᵐ. Rigel is located at a distance of approximately 860 light years from the Sun.

Antares

Antares or Antares is the brightest star in the constellation Scorpio and one of the brightest stars in the night sky, a red supergiant. It is located at a distance of about 550 light years from the Sun. In Russia, it is perfectly visible in the southern regions, but it is also observed in the central ones.
