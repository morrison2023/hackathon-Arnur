Satellite

A satellite is an object in celestial mechanics that revolves along a certain trajectory (orbit) around another object.Sometimes the name "Cosmos" was assigned to vehicles that in other cases had their own names according to the relevant programs, for example, during tests and flights in the unmanned version of the Soyuz spacecraft of various options, the L1 / Zond lunar flyby spacecraft, the prototype lunar orbital spacecraft LOK of the lunar landing expeditionary complex L3, the TKS transport module, a reduced prototype of the orbital spaceplane of the Spiral reusable system.

Express-AM3

Express-AM3 is a Russian telecommunications satellite of the Express series, created by NPO PM together with the French company Alcatel Space, whose transponders are directed mainly to Siberia and the Far East. During the operation of the device, a large number of malfunctions in the operation of service systems were identified.

ASIASAT 2

AsiaSat 2 was a Hong Kong communications satellite that was owned and originally operated by the Hong Kong-based Asia Satellite Tele Communications Company. It was placed in geostationary orbit at longitude 17° east of the Greenwich meridian and leased to Spacecom.

Horizons-2

Horizons-2 is a Kᵤ band communications satellite owned by Horizons Satellite, a joint venture between SKY Perfect JSAT Group and Intelsat. Its orbital slot is located at 74° West longitude.

Intelsat

Intelsat is an international satellite communications organization founded in 1964 as an interstate organization. Later transformed into a commercial company. The Intelsat space constellation for April, 2013 consists of 55 telecommunication satellites. The largest satellite company in the world.
